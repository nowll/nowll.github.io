    New York plastic surgeon's viral TikTok video warns of exercise he says causes premature aging

    A New York plastic surgeon has taken TikTok by storm with a viral video warning about an exercise he claims causes premature aging. Dr. John Smith, renowned for his expertise in cosmetic procedures, has sparked widespread discussion with his controversial assertion that certain high-impact exercises can accelerate the aging process. In his video, Dr. Smith explains that repetitive high-intensity activities, particularly those involving heavy pounding and jarring movements, can lead to increased wear and tear on the body's tissues, contributing to signs of aging such as sagging skin and joint degradation.



    Dr. Smith's video specifically calls out exercises like long-distance running and intense plyometric workouts. According to him, these activities subject the body to significant mechanical stress, which over time can break down collagen and elastin fibers in the skin, leading to a loss of elasticity and firmness. Additionally, he notes that the repeated impact on joints can hasten cartilage deterioration, making individuals more prone to conditions like osteoarthritis. His advice has resonated with many viewers, prompting a reevaluation of their fitness routines in favor of lower-impact alternatives.



    In response to Dr. Smith's claims, fitness experts have weighed in, emphasizing the importance of a balanced approach to exercise. While acknowledging the potential risks of high-impact activities, they stress that the benefits of regular physical activity far outweigh the negatives when performed correctly and in moderation. They recommend incorporating a variety of exercises, including low-impact options such as swimming, cycling, and yoga, to maintain overall health and fitness without overstraining the body.
